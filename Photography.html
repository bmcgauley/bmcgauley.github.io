Portfolio: Photography

Project: 4th of July Fireworks

Objective: Take photographs of clients fireworks for a 4th of July celebration

Synopsis:

Used a Nikon D700 with a kit lense to capture the explosive display of fireworks by the client as memorabilia for them to keep for a family keepsake.

Kit Used

  • Nikon D700
  • Nikon DX AF-S NIKKOR 18-55mm 1:3.5-5.6G Kit Lense
  • Full manual ISO, exposure, shutter speed, focus, ect.
  • Tripod was used
  • Photo cleanup was done in Adobe Lightroom

Challenges:

  • Dusk time exposure was extremely hard to keep up with given the rapid loss of light during the event
  • Focus was hard to maintain given the proximity of fireworks in relation to the camera and the unpredictability of them
  • Exposure was extremely difficult to manage between ambient light and emitted light from fireworks at any given time
  • Due to longer exposures and rapid movement of firework particles, streaks and streams were hard to manage and control (especially with black powder dust)
  • Tripod management and lense width was hard to manage given the narrow angle this lense provided

Retrospective:

What went wrong?

  • Most shots were unusable due to improper exposure, heavy streaking of firework particles, and many other factors from above
  • Tripod management and shot area management led to missing multiple good shots
  • Most artifacts and issues were not found until touch-ups in Lightroom, due to the resolution of the D700 screen
  • Did not have an appropriate lense for the angle needed to capture the full view of the fireworks

What went right?

  • Fired off a large quantity of shots even if I was unsure of the shot, leading to far more usable shots in the end
  • Exposure and shutter speed, despite the difficulty, were well managed across all shots
  • Due to proper exposure and shutter speed, clipping was extremely minimal during the editing process
  • Given the amount of challenges and roadblocks, some shots were the best shots I've ever taken
