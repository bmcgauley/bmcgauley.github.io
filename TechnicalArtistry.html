Why Aliens?

Using aliens we were able to use colors, contrast, branch styles and leaf styles to convey the genetic concepts of mandelian genetics to students.

Environment

Coordinate and plan effeciently to blend aliens into the scenery using natural camouflage

to provide students a small challenge

Auxillary Environment props

Using trim-sheets for textures, created a wide variaty of props for the alien habitat to be used

"by" the aliens, including tables, chairs, tree platforms, cups, pots, ect.

Lore

Helped to coordinate and accurately display the aliens habitat and culture and lore

through auxillary prop creation and alterations.

Planning

Tightly working with design for feedback and iteration to refine multiple styles for a wide array

of genetic alterations to the base alien.

Greyboxing & Silhouettes

Using orthographic techniques, utilized a wide variety of tools to quickly iterate and rapid prototype dozens

of styles for multiple teams to give feedback including design, marketing and development teams.

Project: Alien Customization

Course Module: Mendelian Genetics

Objective: Take a premade alien asset and convert to usable variations with many other customizations to conform to the needs of course Module

Synopsis:

Solely in charge of various tasks to convert a ready alien asset into a usable form for Mandelian Genetics. Breakdown will be in 2 parts; pure asset generation and asset adaptation.

Asset Generation:

  • Roughly a dozen different headset branch "hairstyles" for level 1 distinction
  • Two different back piece branch patterns for level 1 distinction
  • One pair of hip branches for level 1 distinction
  • branches fully rigged to asset skeleton and made to be compatible within Unity Game Engine
  • Two different types of leaf patterns to append to branches for level 2 distinction
  • Two variations of leaf color for level 2 distinction

Asset Adaptation:

  • Adapted rigged skeleton while making adjustments to fix various issues with weight painting within 3ds Max
  • Completley revamped base alien texture and customized to allow for 3 base variations with 2 secondary variations for level 3 distinction (elderly, child, adult)
  • Custom eye material/texture to invite a friendlier atmosphere amongst the aliens and to be less intimidating to students
  • Severl variations of color to eye texture/emissive for level 2 distinction
  • conformed skeleton rig to allow for smaller aliens as children was a requirement

Retrospective:

What went wrong?

  • Being the sole developer on this project contributed to slight delays
  • Had to redo some work due to over-iteration (extensive greyboxing / silhouettes) in the design phase
  • Some oversight on environment placement lead to template color changing multiple times

What went right?

  • Working modularily contributed to a total net gain in overall productivity
  • Despites some downsides of overplanning, this cut a huge workload off of the total time it would have taken to complete this many variations
  • Was able to leveral many different skillsets in varying degrees to deliver a very high quality asset bundle
