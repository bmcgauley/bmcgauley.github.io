Portfolio: 3D Printing

Project: Back to the Future Party Props

Objective: Create two main decoration items for a Back to the Future themed event

Synopsis:

Created the iconic clocktower from Back to the Future as well as the base for the coffee maker that mounts to the Delorean, both ready for 3d Print.

Resources

  • Zbrush for creation & slice planning
  • Custom PLA Filament 3d printer
  • Cura 3d for slice verification & support planning
  • Custom 3d print software for actual printing
  • PLA Filament

Challenges:

  • The biggest challenge for both pieces was definitely the size they needed to be printed at
  • Finding slice sections that were logical and easily hideable post-production
  • Resource management was a definite challenge, getting a high quality print with as little material as possible
  • Detail was a challenge due to the use of PLA instead of resin, due to the scale and density of the print
  • Finer details were hard to get right (the clock itself, as well as the figures) and had to be highly customized to work for PLA print

Retrospective:

What went wrong?

  • Detail wasn't as fine as I would have liked given the thickness of the PLA print
  • Slice segments and sections could have been planned better for lock-fitting instead of glueing
  • Quick turnaround requirement limited much of the in depth scope that could have been given (eg lock-fitting)

What went right?

  • Material management was a major concern given the size and scale of both pieces. Both pieces were managed well in terms of material reduction on the asset creation portion
  • Detail, while wasn't perfect or near the quality of resin printing, turned out perfect for the needs of this project given the usage of PLA filament
  • Both main assets were delivered on time despite the hard deadline of the event
